Black Art-Chives

Black art is beauty.Express your beauty in black art.

Black Art-Chive is an art gallery that promotes African American art work.Through art-work, we strive to spread peace, unity, and love to all, especially African American communities. We inform, educate and engage communities in African American history and culture by way of art gallery shows, motivational and insightful speakers, art classes, and the display of art-work created by some of the most talented local and world wide African American artist.

We provide platforms to African-American artist where they can display their art and people can by the art work. We give art classes to help people brush up on their art skills or become starting artist if they have a passion for it or just want to have fun. We host events with public speakers who share their stories or expertise in specific topic areas of interest. People in the community can attend events to gain knowledge, information, network or just enjoy themselves.

About Us

Black Art-Chives was founded in 2018 and is located in Atlanta, Georgia. Founders Lyric McGhee, Venise Ivey and Kendarious Eason focused their efforts in bringing awareness and unity to African Americans through art. They believed if people could see the love and beauty around them, it would make them see it in themselves and spread it to everyone one person at a time. They each had a hand in helping build the art gallery from ground up combining their visions into one. In starting Black Art-Chives, they partnered with nonprofits that focused on helping youth in poverty stricken African American communities. Raising Expectations and Black Youth Project are two proud partners of Black Art-Chives. Black Art-Chives look forward to building in different locations in the near future to give more access to African American art work and be platforms for African American artists in different areas.

Briyona James

Brionya is an upcoming artist from Austin, TX who has expanded her gift internationally across the globe.She considers herself a God Taught Artist, allowing the spirit to express itself on canvas. Brionya's inspiration is derived from the experiences of life, conversations, revelations, and many forms of creative expressions that ignite a vision into a visual story told. Brionya tends to correlate her poems with each piece so the viewer can reflect and perceive their own notion to connect to it.

Kehinde Wiley

Kehinde Wiley was born 1977. He is a New York City-based portrait painter who is known for his highly naturalistic paintings of African-Americans. The Columbus Museum of Art, which hosted an exhibition of his work in 2007, describes his work as follows: "Wiley has gained recent acclaim for his heroic portraits which address the image and status of young African-American men in contemporary culture."[2]

Clarence Howard

Clarence Howard is an up and rising photographer in from Atlanta, Georgia. His photography is his artwork in which he is inspired by his city and African American roots. He uses his photography as bases for creating art through painting realistic and dynamic pieces.
